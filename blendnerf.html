3D-aware Blending with Generative NeRFs

Hyunsu Kim1, Gayoung Lee1, Yunjey Choi1, Jin-Hwa Kim1,2, Jun-Yan Zhu3
1NAVER AI Lab, 2SNU AIIS, 3CMU
Paper (High res) arXiv Code

BlendNeRF can align and compose unaligned images without manual effort.

Abstract

Image blending is a long-standing problem to naturally combine two images. While multiple works have been proposed to solve it, they severely underperform when input images are not well-aligned in terms of rotation, scale and translation. In this work, we propose a 3D-aware blending method for misaligned images using generative Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF).

Our method consists of two stages: 3D-aware alignment and 3D-aware blending. For 3D-aware alignment, we first globally align the reference image by using the pretrained generative NeRF with the inversion method, and then locally align each part using the warping algorithm. To take advantage of 3D information for blending, we propose 3D-aware blending that directly blends images on the NeRF space.

We demonstrate that our 3D-aware blending outperforms the competitive baselines validating with extensive quantitative and qualitative evaluations with FFHQ and AFHQ-Cat.
